¿How to make a Lasagna?

Lasagna

Lasagna is a type of pasta. It is usually served in overlapping sheets interspersed with layers of ingredients to taste, most frequently meat in bolognese sauce and bechamel.

  • 10-12 pre-cooked lasagna plates
  • 600 g minced beef
  • 1 onion
  • 2 carrots
  • 2 stalks of celery
  • 500 g canned chopped tomato (or whole peeled tomato)
  • 500 ml of water
  • 80 g of grated cheese (cheddar, Parmesan or other)
  • Salt and ground pepper to taste
  • Extra virgin olive oil

Steps to make a lasgna

  1. Place a little olive oil in a saucepan or cocotte on the heat and add the minced meat with salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Keep moving and separating the meat with the heat quite high.
  3. Meanwhile, cut the onion, carrots and celery into very small pieces so that they blend well into the sauce.
  4. To do it very quickly we have used the Gefu manual chopper.
  5. When the meat is browned, add the onion, carrot and celery.
  6. When the meat is browned, add the onion, carrot and celery. Cook for 8-10 minutes over medium heat. Add the chopped tomato and water, stir well and let it reduce for 1 hour, or until you achieve the consistency you want for your filling. Booking.
  7. While the sauce rests we are going to prepare the bechamel.
  8. Heat 3 tablespoons of oil in a frying pan and add 4 tablespoons of flour. Stir very well to toast the flour a little.
  9. Remove the pan from the heat and add the hot milk little by little while stirring with a hand whisk.
  10. Add a pinch of black pepper, another pinch of nutmeg and a little salt.
  11. When everything is integrated, put it back on the heat and cook without stopping stirring for 5-7 minutes over low heat, or until the sauce has thickened a little. For lasagna, a bechamel that is not too thick is better.
  12. If you have lumps left, you can fix it with a touch of a mixer.
  13. To assemble the lasagna you need a baking dish of about 30 x 20 cm. Put a few tablespoons of meat filling at the base of the dish so that the lasagna does not stick.
  14. On top, place the pasta plates, a well-spread layer of meat and a few tablespoons of béchamel.
  15. Repeat this process two more times.
  16. Finish the lasagna with a layer of pasta sheets and cover with the bechamel and grated cheese on top.
