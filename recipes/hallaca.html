¿How to make a Hallaca?

Hallaca

Hallaca is a traditional dish from Venezuela. It has been compared to the tamale. It is made from a corn flour dough, seasoned with chicken or chicken broth and dyed with onoto or achiote, filled with a stew of beef, pork and chicken or chicken.

  • 2 kg. of chicken.
  • 2 kg. of beef.
  • 1 kg. of pork.
  • 1 kg. of diced onions.
  • 400 gr. of finely chopped leek garlic.
  • 200 gr. of finely chopped chives.
  • ¾ cup peeled and crushed garlic cloves.
  • 1 kg. paprika, seeded and cut into thin strips.
  • ¼ cup finely chopped sweet pepper.
  • ½ cup capers.
  • 500 gr. of ground paper.
  • Worcestershire sauce to taste.
  • 1 ½ kg. of tomatoes without skin and without seeds.
  • 1 cup of sweet wine.
  • 1 ½ cup vegetable oil.
  • 2 cups of broth.
  • 2 ½ kgs. of yellow corn flour.
  • 1 cup of vegetable oil.
  • 3 liters of chicken broth.

Steps to make a hallaca

Of the stew
  1. Wash the meat very well and remove excess fat.
  2. Parboil each piece separately, without softening them completely.
  4. The meat must be firm to prevent it from falling apart when preparing the stew.
  5. Reserve the broth that results from cooking the beef and chicken.
  6. Separately, in a large pot, sauté onion, garlic, chives and garlic, and sauté for 5 minutes or until wilted.
  7. Add the paprika, sweet chili and 2 cups of broth.
  8. Let cook for a few more minutes.
  9. Add the meats, and add the rest of the ingredients, leaving finally the tomatoes, wine and marinade to taste.
  10. Cook the stew for 30 to 40 minutes, stirring occasionally, being careful to prevent it from falling apart.
  11. Cook the stew until it thickens and let it rest, covered with a cloth, overnight.
of the dough
  1. Knead the corn flour with the oil and broth.
  2. Form balls of 5 or 7 cm with your hands. approximately.
of the hallacas
  1. Grease a large sheet with the onoto-colored oil.
  2. In the center, place a ball of dough and flatten it firmly until the dough is very thin, about 1 to 2 millimeters thick, uniform and in the shape of a circle. Then, pour 2 ½ tablespoons of the cold stew into the center of the dough.
  3. On top, place the decorations evenly distributed: 2 paprika strips, 2 olives and 6 raisins; The amount depends on taste.
  4. You can also add pickles to the filling.
  5. To wrap them, fold the sides successively and then the two ends inward.
  6. Wrap first with the large leaf, then with the smaller leaf, as a protective wrap against water.
  7. Tighten carefully and tie with wick, crossing it 3 times in each direction. It should always end in the center so that the mooring is more secure.
  8. Finally, in a pot with boiling water and salt, place the hallacas. Let them cook for 1 hour.
  9. Remove from the water and let them drain.
  10. Serve hot.
  11. Once cool, refrigerate the hallacas that are not consumed, so they will remain in good condition for up to 4 weeks.
